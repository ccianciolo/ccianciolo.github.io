TopSpin

Tennis Notes,
Simplified.

Growing up in Maine focusing on tennis as a sport is a challenge.
Cost aside, tennis is an easy sport to get into, but is difficult to
master. Tennis coaches in the area guide students to use a tennis
notebook to gather notes and keep track of their daily goals but it’s
a challenge to get this routine to stick. TopSpin Cloud aims to
meet the needs of both coaches and players alike by allowing for
collaboration and tracking of progress in an easy to use
iOS application.

View XD Prototype

The Quest

The cloud storage and organization market is young and ripe for development.
Even with big name companies filling the space there is room for another
player to meet specific needs and enter the fray. Identifying a need in an
already competitive market provided a challenge to overcome. The client
identified there is potential to do something new in the cloud storage market
but did not give much information beyond that. It was up to me, the UX
researcher and designer to laser focus my efforts by gathering data and
massaging it into a product design to bring to market.

Competitive Analysis

I did a competitive analysis of some of the larger competitors in the cloud storage
market to see what was already available. The market is full of different competitors
doing very different types of things. Each of the cloud services that were chosen for
analysis were identified specifically because they are utilized for different purposes.
Each of them saves content to the cloud.

Google Drive stores all sorts of files and is fantastic for collaboration with others.
Drive allows for collaboration in real time within content that can be created from within
the Google Drive platform.

Pinterest saves links to content and is great for sharing creative “pins”. It is a great
place to gain insight into new ideas or gather creative ideas when initially embarking on
a project.

Evernote is a note taking application that saves content from the web that is stored
within the service. Evernote allows for collaboration as well and has the ability to take
photos, record audio and add a sketch along with attaching files to a “note”. Organization
within Evernote is limited to a notebook style system.

There are many questions that will need to be asked and through user surveys and analysis of
this data a niche market will begin to take shape.

User Research

Initially I decided to work with what I know. In my current job, I work with teachers
who have a wide range of needs. I began the process by surveying teachers and tennis
players about their cloud storage habits and needs. Teaching, and teaching tennis are two
subjects that I know a lot about being a former Director of Tennis and a current Technology
Coach working with k-12 teachers. I decided to survey these two areas to work with what I know.

A majority (94%) of the surveyed use Google drive as it is what has been provided to
them by their school district or work environment. Knowing that teachers use cloud
storage was the foundation, but I needed to drill down deeper to see what needs they
may have that could be met by a new solution in the market. The survey data
identified some areas that would need to be addressed; organization methods and
search ease.

At this point I had quality data, but not enough to narrow down the focus of my
application design. I designed two user personas based on the demographic data
provided through the survey and brainstormed with my team lead.

In our conversation we agreed that I seemed more passionate about the Tennis
player and teacher demographic but needed data to confirm that instinct. I had
an inlking of where this data would go but wanted to back it up with solid info
so I developed another 5 question survey.

In this second survey I was able to identify that there was in fact a need that
was not being met for tennis players and coaches.

My instinct seemed to be correct as a need was identified and an interest was
shown in a potential note taking app geared specifically towards tennis players.

Upon refocusing on a tennis goal and note taking app I did a quick competitive
analysis taking a peek at Google Keep to see what the competition was doing in
the realm of note taking and where there are areas for entrance into the market I
also delved deeper into the user personas to identify the two major users for this
app, tennis players and coaches.

Quest Completed

This research really determined the direction of the app and any processes and
design stemmed from it. Details, details, details are what I learned from
designing this application. My initial designs were done as PNG files from
Affinity Designer which was great to learn, but if I had it to do over again
I would have used XD for the whole project from the start. There is something
freeing about just making little tweaks within one piece of software.
In my wireframe process I used both Affinity Designer and Adobe XD to develop the
prototype. This was a learning experience because there was a lot of time spent
iterating in Affinity and then exporting and pulling into Adobe XD. Also once the
files were inputted into Adobe to make changes required going back into Designer
OR adding clickable rectangles over the top as “white out” and then adding a new
element inside of XD. Some of the more visual elements needed to be designed in
Affinity but when designing so many different screens I think Adobe makes things
more efficient. All of the information gathered directed decisions I made throughout
the process. The end result was a research based coherent design that can be
implemented for design onto the iOS app store.
